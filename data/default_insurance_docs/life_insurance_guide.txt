LIFE INSURANCE - COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE (INDIA)

================================================================================
SECTION 1: LIFE INSURANCE BASICS
================================================================================

What is Life Insurance?

Life insurance is a contract between you (policyholder) and an insurance company where the insurer promises to pay a designated beneficiary (nominee) a sum of money upon your death, in exchange for regular premium payments.

Regulatory Body: Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI)

Purpose of Life Insurance:
- Income replacement for dependents
- Debt repayment (home loan, car loan)
- Children's education and marriage
- Retirement corpus
- Estate planning
- Tax savings

Life Insurance Penetration in India: 3.2% of GDP
(Global average: 3.0%)

Tax Benefits:

Section 80C - Premium Payment:
- Deduction up to ₹1,50,000 per year
- For self, spouse, and children

Section 80D - Health Riders:
- Additional ₹25,000 - ₹50,000
- If health riders attached

Section 10(10D) - Maturity Proceeds:
- Tax-free maturity if premium ≤ 10% of sum assured
- For policies issued after 01.04.2012

SECTION 2: TYPES OF LIFE INSURANCE
================================================================================

Term Insurance (Pure Protection):

What it is:
- Pure death benefit
- No maturity value
- Most affordable option

Features:
- High coverage at low premium
- Death benefit to nominees
- No survival benefit
- Flexible policy terms (10-40 years)

Who should buy:
- Primary earning member
- Anyone with financial dependents
- People with large debts

Sum Assured Range: ₹25,00,000 - ₹10,00,00,000
Premium Range: ₹5,000 - ₹25,000 per year
(For ₹1 crore cover, 30-year-old non-smoker)

Whole Life Insurance:

What it is:
- Coverage for entire lifetime
- Premium payment for limited period
- Guaranteed death benefit

Features:
- Lifetime protection (up to age 99-100)
- Higher premium than term
- May have cash value component

Premium: 2-3x of term insurance

Endowment Plans:

What it is:
- Insurance + Savings combined
- Guaranteed maturity benefit
- Bonus additions over years

Features:
- Death benefit OR maturity benefit
- Annual bonuses declared
- Lower returns (4-6%)
- Higher premiums

Variants:
- Traditional endowment
- Money-back plans
- Anticipated endowment

Sum Assured: ₹5,00,000 - ₹50,00,000
Premium: ₹20,000 - ₹1,00,000 per year

Unit Linked Insurance Plans (ULIPs):

What it is:
- Insurance + Market investment
- Premium invested in equity/debt funds
- Market-linked returns

Features:
- Flexibility in fund selection
- Partial withdrawals after 5 years
- Fund switching allowed
- Lock-in period: 5 years

Types of Funds:
- Equity fund: High risk, high return
- Balanced fund: Medium risk
- Debt fund: Low risk, stable return

Sum Assured: 10x annual premium (minimum)
Charges: Fund management, mortality, admin

Child Plans:

What it is:
- Education and marriage fund
- Waiver of premium on parent's death
- Guaranteed payouts

Features:
- Regular payouts at milestones
- Policy continues if parent dies
- Goal-based planning

Common Milestones:
- Age 18: College education
- Age 21: Higher education
- Age 25: Career/marriage

Sum Assured: ₹10,00,000 - ₹50,00,000

Pension/Annuity Plans:

What it is:
- Retirement income planning
- Regular income after retirement
- Annuity payment options

Types:
- Deferred annuity (accumulation phase)
- Immediate annuity (instant income)

Annuity Options:
- Life annuity: Income for lifetime
- Joint life: Income for you and spouse
- With return: Capital returned on death
- Guaranteed period: Fixed years guaranteed

Annuity Rates: 5-8% per annum

Money-Back Policies:

What it is:
- Regular payouts during term
- Survival benefits at intervals
- Full sum assured on death

Features:
- Liquidity through periodic payouts
- Useful for planned expenses
- Lower effective returns

Payout Example (20-year policy):
- After 5 years: 20% of sum assured
- After 10 years: 20% of sum assured
- After 15 years: 20% of sum assured
- At maturity: 40% + bonuses

SECTION 3: TERM INSURANCE IN DETAIL
================================================================================

Why Term Insurance is Recommended:

Cost Comparison (₹1 crore cover, Age 30, 30 years):
- Term Insurance: ₹8,000 - ₹15,000/year
- Endowment: ₹2,50,000 - ₹4,00,000/year
- ULIP: ₹1,00,000 - ₹2,00,000/year

Key Features to Look For:

1. Claim Settlement Ratio:
   - Above 95% is excellent
   - Check IRDAI published data
   - Top insurers: 98-99%

2. Sum Assured Options:
   - Level cover: Same throughout
   - Increasing cover: Grows 5-10% yearly
   - Decreasing cover: For loan repayment

3. Premium Payment Options:
   - Regular pay: Throughout term
   - Limited pay: 5, 7, 10, 15 years
   - Single pay: One-time premium

4. Policy Term Options:
   - 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, 35, 40 years
   - Cover till age 60, 65, 70, 75, 80, 85

Add-On Riders:

Accidental Death Benefit:
- Additional payout on accidental death
- Usually equal to sum assured
- Cost: ₹500 - ₹1,500/year per crore

Accidental Disability Benefit:
- Monthly income on permanent disability
- Waiver of future premiums
- Cost: ₹800 - ₹2,000/year

Critical Illness Cover:
- Lump sum on diagnosis
- Covers 30+ critical conditions
- Cost: ₹3,000 - ₹8,000/year per ₹25 lakh

Waiver of Premium:
- Premiums waived on disability
- Policy continues without payment
- Cost: ₹500 - ₹1,000/year

Terminal Illness Benefit:
- Early payout if diagnosed terminal
- Usually included free
- Payout: 50-100% of sum assured

How Much Cover Do You Need?

Human Life Value Method:
Cover = (Annual Income × Years to Retirement) - Existing Assets + Liabilities

Example:
Annual Income: ₹12,00,000
Years to Retirement: 30
Factor: 10x income minimum
Required Cover: ₹1,20,00,000 (₹1.2 crore)

Add:
- Outstanding home loan: ₹40,00,000
- Car loan: ₹5,00,000
- Children's education: ₹30,00,000
- Marriage expenses: ₹20,00,000

Total Cover Needed: ₹2,15,00,000 (₹2.15 crore)

Premium Factors:

1. Age:
   - Younger = Lower premium
   - Buy early to lock low rates

2. Smoking Status:
   - Non-smoker: Standard rates
   - Smoker: 30-50% higher premium

3. Health Conditions:
   - Pre-existing diseases: Loading
   - BMI: High BMI may increase premium

4. Occupation:
   - Desk jobs: Standard
   - Hazardous: Additional loading

5. Sum Assured:
   - Higher cover: Lower rate per lakh
   - Volume discounts available

6. Policy Term:
   - Longer term: Higher total premium
   - But same annual rate

SECTION 5: CLAIM PROCESS
================================================================================

Death Claim Process:

Immediate Steps:
1. Inform insurer within 24-48 hours
2. Get death certificate
3. Collect policy documents

Required Documents:
- Claim form (from insurer)
- Original policy document
- Death certificate
- Hospital records (if applicable)
- FIR (for accidental death)
- Post-mortem report (if applicable)
- Nominee ID proof
- Cancelled cheque
- NEFT details

Investigation Cases:
Claims investigated if:
- Death within 3 years of policy
- Suicide within 1 year
- Non-disclosure of material facts
- Large sum assured

Claim Timeline:
- Standard claims: 15-30 days
- Investigated claims: 90-180 days
- IRDAI mandated: 30 days for decision

Maturity Claim Process:

Automatic Processing:
- Insurer sends intimation before maturity
- Submit survival certificate
- Provide bank details
- Amount credited directly

Required Documents:
- Original policy document
- Identity proof
- Address proof
- Cancelled cheque
- Discharge voucher (if any)

Survival Benefit Claims:

For money-back policies:
- Automatic reminder from insurer
- Submit survival certificate
- Receive payout as per schedule

SECTION 6: IMPORTANT TERMS
================================================================================

Sum Assured:

The guaranteed amount payable:
- On death: To nominee
- On maturity: To policyholder
- Minimum/maximum limits apply

Premium:

Types:
- Annual: Once a year
- Half-yearly: Twice a year
- Quarterly: Four times a year
- Monthly: Every month (usually ECS)

Grace Period:
- Annual premium: 30 days
- Monthly premium: 15 days

Policy Term:

The duration of coverage:
- Term insurance: 10-40 years
- Endowment: 10-30 years
- ULIP: 10-30 years (5-year lock-in)

Nominee:

The beneficiary who receives death benefit:
- Can be any person
- Minor needs appointee
- Multiple nominees allowed
- Percentage split possible

Revival of Lapsed Policy:

If premium not paid:
- Policy lapses after grace period
- Can revive within 2-5 years
- Pay all due premiums with interest
- Medical examination may be required

Surrender Value:

Cash value if policy surrendered:
- Guaranteed Surrender Value (GSV)
- Special Surrender Value (SSV)
- Available after 3 years premium paid
- Much lower than premiums paid

Paid-Up Value:

If you stop paying premiums:
- Reduced cover continues
- No more premiums required
- Lower maturity/death benefit

Free Look Period:

15-30 days to review policy:
- Full refund if cancelled
- Deductions for stamp duty and medical
- Starts from policy receipt date

SECTION 7: TAX IMPLICATIONS
================================================================================

Section 80C Deduction:

Eligible Premiums:
- Life insurance premium
- Maximum ₹1,50,000 per year
- For self, spouse, children

Condition:
- Premium ≤ 10% of sum assured
- For policies after 01.04.2012

Section 10(10D) Exemption:

Maturity Proceeds Tax-Free if:
- Premium ≤ 10% of sum assured
- Death benefit always tax-free

New Rules (Budget 2023):

For policies after 01.04.2023:
- Premium > ₹5,00,000/year
- Maturity proceeds taxable
- Term insurance exempt

Section 80CCC:

Pension Plans:
- Premium deduction up to ₹1,50,000
- Combined with 80C limit

Annuity Taxation:

- Annuity income taxable
- Added to total income
- Taxed as per slab

SECTION 9: TIPS FOR BUYING LIFE INSURANCE
================================================================================

1. Buy Early:
   - Lower premiums at young age
   - Easier underwriting
   - Lock in rates for life

2. Adequate Coverage:
   - Minimum 10-15x annual income
   - Account for inflation
   - Include liabilities

3. Compare Online:
   - Use aggregators
   - Direct website purchase cheaper
   - No intermediary commission

4. Check Claim Settlement Ratio:
   - Above 95% is good
   - Prefer 98%+ insurers
   - Check IRDAI website

5. Read Policy Document:
   - Understand exclusions
   - Know claim process
   - Review terms carefully

6. Nominate Correctly:
   - Update nominee after life events
   - Include percentage split
   - Appoint for minor nominees

7. Disclose Everything:
   - Medical history
   - Lifestyle habits
   - Occupation details
   - Non-disclosure can void claim

8. Review Annually:
   - Update coverage for life changes
   - Marriage, children, income increase
   - Buy additional cover if needed

9. Don't Mix Investment:
   - Term insurance for protection
   - Separate investments (MF, PPF, NPS)
   - Better returns and flexibility

10. Add Riders Wisely:
    - Critical illness: Important
    - Accidental death: Consider
    - Waiver of premium: Useful
