INSURANCE GLOSSARY - INDIAN INSURANCE TERMS

================================================================================
GENERAL INSURANCE TERMS
================================================================================

Actuary (एक्चुअरी):
A professional who uses mathematics, statistics, and financial theory to study 
uncertain future events, especially in insurance and pension programs. 
Determines premium rates and reserves.

Add-On Cover / Rider (ऐड-ऑन कवर):
Additional coverage purchased along with base policy for extra protection.
Examples: Zero depreciation, engine protect, critical illness rider.
Typically costs ₹500 - ₹5,000 extra per year.

Agent (एजेंट):
Licensed individual who sells insurance policies on behalf of insurance company.
Must pass IRDAI examination. Earns commission on policy sales.

Annuity (वार्षिकी):
Regular payment received from an insurance company, typically after retirement.
Types: Immediate annuity, deferred annuity.
Rates in India: 5-8% per annum.

Assignment (समनुदेशन):
Transfer of policy rights from one person to another.
Used for loan collateral or ownership transfer.

Beneficiary (लाभार्थी):
Person designated to receive insurance benefits.
Also called nominee in life insurance.

Bonus (बोनस):
Additional amount added to life insurance sum assured.
Types: Simple reversionary bonus, terminal bonus.
Declared annually by insurer.

Cashless Claim (कैशलेस क्लेम):
Claim settlement where insurer pays hospital/garage directly.
Policyholder pays only deductible or non-covered items.
Available at network hospitals/garages.

Claim (दावा):
Formal request to insurance company for payment under policy terms.
Must be filed within specified time limit.
Documents required for processing.

Claim Settlement Ratio (क्लेम सेटलमेंट रेश्यो):
Percentage of claims paid vs claims received.
Higher ratio indicates better claim payment record.
Published by IRDAI annually.
Good ratio: Above 95%

Co-Insurance (सह-बीमा):
Sharing of risk between multiple insurers for large risks.
Also refers to shared payment in health insurance.

Co-Payment / Co-Pay (सह-भुगतान):
Fixed percentage of claim amount payable by policyholder.
Common in health insurance: 10%, 20%, 30%
Example: ₹1,00,000 bill with 20% co-pay = You pay ₹20,000

Compulsory Deductible (अनिवार्य कटौती):
Mandatory amount policyholder must pay before claim.
Set by IRDAI for motor insurance.
Private car: ₹1,000, Two-wheeler: ₹100

Coverage / Cover (कवरेज):
Protection provided by insurance policy.
Scope of risks insured against.

Deductible (कटौती):
Amount you pay before insurance kicks in.
Higher deductible = Lower premium.
Example: ₹5,000 deductible means you pay first ₹5,000 of any claim.

Depreciation (मूल्यह्रास):
Reduction in value due to wear and tear.
Applied to motor insurance claims.
Year 1: 5%, Year 2: 10%, Year 3: 15% (for plastic parts)

Endorsement (पृष्ठांकन):
Written amendment to policy terms.
Changes coverage, premium, or policyholder details.
Issued during policy period.

Exclusions (अपवर्जन):
Risks or situations NOT covered by policy.
Listed in policy document.
Important to read before buying.

First Party (प्रथम पक्ष):
The insured person or policyholder.
Claims made for own losses.

Free Look Period (फ्री लुक पीरियड):
Period to review and return policy.
Usually 15-30 days from receipt.
Full refund (minus charges) if cancelled.

Grace Period (अनुग्रह अवधि):
Extra time to pay premium after due date.
Policy remains active during this period.
Life insurance: 30 days (annual), 15 days (monthly)
General insurance: Usually 30 days

Group Insurance (समूह बीमा):
Single policy covering multiple people.
Common for employees, associations.
Lower premium per person.

================================================================================
LIFE INSURANCE TERMS
================================================================================

Assured / Life Assured (बीमित):
Person whose life is insured under the policy.
Benefits paid on death of life assured.

Death Benefit (मृत्यु लाभ):
Amount paid to nominee on death of life assured.
Equals sum assured plus accumulated bonuses.

Endowment Policy (एंडोमेंट पॉलिसी):
Life insurance with savings component.
Pays on death OR maturity, whichever earlier.
Returns: 4-6% typically.

Human Life Value (HLV) (मानव जीवन मूल्य):
Economic value of a person based on earning capacity.
Used to determine adequate life cover.
Formula: Annual income × Years to retirement

Lapsed Policy (व्यपगत पॉलिसी):
Policy terminated due to non-payment of premium.
Can be revived within specified period (2-5 years).
Revival requires payment of arrears plus interest.

Maturity (परिपक्वता):
End of policy term when benefits become payable.
Maturity benefit = Sum assured + bonuses.
Tax-free under Section 10(10D) if conditions met.

Money-Back Policy (मनी-बैक पॉलिसी):
Periodic payouts during policy term.
Survival benefits at regular intervals.
Lower effective returns than endowment.

Mortality Charge (मृत्यु दर प्रभार):
Cost of life insurance protection.
Deducted in ULIPs and term insurance.
Increases with age.

NAV (Net Asset Value):
Value per unit of ULIP fund.
Calculated daily based on fund portfolio.
NAV = (Total Assets - Liabilities) / Number of Units

No Claim Bonus (NCB) - Life:
Increase in sum assured for claim-free years.
Health insurance: 5-50% cumulative increase.

Nominee (नामांकित व्यक्ति):
Person designated to receive death benefit.
Can be changed anytime during policy term.
If minor, appointee must be named.

Paid-Up Policy (प्रदत्त पॉलिसी):
Policy where premium payment stops but coverage continues.
Reduced sum assured based on premiums paid.
Benefits proportionately reduced.

Policy Term (पॉलिसी अवधि):
Duration of insurance coverage.
Term insurance: 10-40 years.
Endowment: 10-30 years.

Premium (प्रीमियम):
Amount paid to insurer for coverage.
Payment modes: Annual, half-yearly, quarterly, monthly.
Can be single pay or regular pay.

Proposer (प्रस्तावक):
Person who applies for insurance.
May be same as life assured or different.
Legally bound by policy terms.

Revival (पुनरुद्धार):
Reinstating a lapsed policy.
Requires payment of dues plus interest.
May need fresh medical examination.

Rider (राइडर):
Additional benefit attached to base policy.
Examples: Accidental death, critical illness.
Extra premium charged.

Sum Assured (बीमित राशि):
Guaranteed amount payable under policy.
Base amount before bonuses.
Death benefit never less than sum assured.

Surrender (समर्पण):
Termination of policy before maturity.
Surrender value paid to policyholder.
Usually much less than premiums paid.
Minimum 3 years premium required.

Surrender Value (समर्पण मूल्य):
Cash value if policy terminated early.
GSV: Guaranteed Surrender Value.
SSV: Special Surrender Value.

Term Insurance (टर्म बीमा):
Pure protection, no maturity benefit.
Death benefit only if insured dies during term.
Most affordable life insurance.
Example: ₹1 crore for ₹10,000/year.

ULIP (Unit Linked Insurance Plan):
Insurance cum investment product.
Premium invested in market-linked funds.
5-year lock-in period.
Returns depend on market performance.

Underwriting (अंडरराइटिंग):
Risk assessment process by insurer.
Determines premium and acceptance.
Based on age, health, lifestyle, occupation.

Vesting Age (निहित आयु):
Age when policy benefits become payable.
Usually 18 years for child policies.
60-70 years for pension policies.

================================================================================
HEALTH INSURANCE TERMS
================================================================================

Cashless Hospitalization:
Treatment at network hospital without upfront payment.
Insurer settles directly with hospital.
Show health card at admission.

Claim Form:
Document to be filled for claim processing.
Available from insurer or TPA.
Must be submitted with supporting documents.

CIBIL Score:
Not directly related to health insurance.
But affects loan-linked insurance products.

Copayment:
Fixed percentage paid by insured.
Common: 10%, 20%
Higher for senior citizens.

Cumulative Bonus:
Increase in sum insured for claim-free years.
Usually 5% per year, up to 50%.
Lost or reduced on making claims.

Day Care Procedure:
Treatment not requiring 24-hour hospitalization.
Covered under health insurance.
Examples: Cataract surgery, dialysis, chemotherapy.

Domiciliary Hospitalization:
Treatment at home when hospital not available.
Covered under some policies.
Conditions apply.

Exclusion Period:
Waiting period for specific conditions.
Pre-existing diseases: 2-4 years.
Specific ailments: 1-2 years.

Family Floater:
Single policy covering entire family.
Shared sum insured among members.
Cost-effective for families.

Hospital Network:
List of hospitals with cashless facility.
Check before buying policy.
Multi-city network preferred.

ICU (Intensive Care Unit):
Critical care unit in hospital.
Higher daily charges.
Sub-limits may apply.

In-Patient:
Person admitted to hospital.
Minimum 24-hour stay for coverage.
Includes room, treatment, medicines.

Incurred Claim Ratio (ICR):
Claims incurred vs premiums earned.
Indicates underwriting performance.
Healthy range: 50-80%.

OPD (Out-Patient Department):
Treatment without hospitalization.
Generally NOT covered.
Some premium policies include OPD.

Pre-Authorization:
Approval from insurer before hospitalization.
Required for cashless claims.
Usually 2-4 hours for approval.

Pre-Existing Disease (PED):
Condition existing before policy purchase.
Waiting period: 2-4 years.
Must be disclosed at purchase.

Pre-Hospitalization:
Expenses before hospital admission.
Usually 30-60 days covered.
Consultations, tests, medicines.

Post-Hospitalization:
Expenses after discharge.
Usually 60-180 days covered.
Follow-up visits, medicines.

Reimbursement:
Payment after policyholder pays hospital.
Submit bills for claim.
Settlement in 15-30 days.

Restoration Benefit:
Sum insured restored if exhausted.
Useful for multiple claims in year.
May be automatic or on fresh illness.

Room Rent Limit:
Maximum daily room charges covered.
Common: ₹3,000-₹10,000 per day.
Or 1-2% of sum insured.
Proportionate deduction if exceeded.

Sub-Limit:
Cap on specific expenses.
Room rent, ICU, specific treatments.
Read carefully in policy.

Sum Insured:
Maximum coverage amount per year.
Recommended: ₹5,00,000 - ₹25,00,000 for metros.
Restore feature increases effective cover.

TPA (Third Party Administrator):
Company managing claims for insurer.
Issues health cards.
Processes cashless claims.

Waiting Period:
Time before coverage becomes active.
Initial waiting: 30 days.
PED waiting: 2-4 years.
Specific disease: 1-2 years.

================================================================================
MOTOR INSURANCE TERMS
================================================================================

Accident Cover:
Protection against accident-related losses.
Personal accident: Injury to self.
Third-party: Injury/damage to others.

Comprehensive Policy:
Covers own damage plus third-party.
Most complete motor coverage.
Includes theft, fire, natural disasters.

IDV (Insured Declared Value):
Maximum claim for total loss/theft.
Based on depreciated vehicle value.
Review and agree at purchase.

Liability Only / Third Party:
Mandatory minimum coverage.
Covers third-party injury/death/damage.
Does NOT cover own vehicle.

NCB (No Claim Bonus):
Discount for claim-free years.
20% after year 1, up to 50% after 5 years.
Transferable to new vehicle/insurer.
Lost if claim made.

Network Garage:
Authorized repair shop.
Cashless repairs available.
Quality assured by insurer.

OD (Own Damage):
Damage to own vehicle.
Separate from third-party cover.
Can be bought standalone (since 2019).

PA (Personal Accident):
Cover for driver/owner injuries.
Mandatory ₹15 lakh for owner-driver.
Optional for passengers.

Zero Depreciation:
Full claim without depreciation deduction.
Ideal for new vehicles (1-5 years).
Higher premium.

REGULATORY AND LEGAL TERMS
================================================================================

IRDAI (Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India):
Insurance sector regulator.
Established: 1999.
Headquarters: Hyderabad.
Functions: Licensing, regulation, consumer protection.

Motor Vehicles Act, 1988:
Law mandating motor insurance.
Third-party insurance compulsory.
Penalties for uninsured vehicles.

Section 80C:
Income Tax deduction for life insurance premium.
Limit: ₹1,50,000 per year.

Section 80D:
Income Tax deduction for health insurance premium.
Self/family: ₹25,000 (₹50,000 for seniors).
Parents: Additional ₹25,000-₹50,000.

Section 10(10D):
Tax exemption on life insurance maturity.
Conditions apply based on premium to sum assured ratio.

Policy Document (पॉलिसी दस्तावेज़):
Legal contract between insurer and insured.
Contains all terms, conditions, exclusions.
Read carefully before signing.

Proposal Form:
Application for insurance.
Contains declarations by proposer.
Basis of policy contract.

Schedule:
Summary page of policy.
Contains key details: Sum insured, premium, period.
Specific terms for the policy.

CURRENCY AND AMOUNT REFERENCES
================================================================================

Standard Format in India:
- Thousand: ₹1,000
- Lakh: ₹1,00,000 (One Lakh = 100,000)
- Crore: ₹1,00,00,000 (One Crore = 10 Million)

Common Insurance Amounts:
- Term Insurance: ₹50,00,000 - ₹10,00,00,000 (50 lakhs - 10 crores)
- Health Insurance: ₹5,00,000 - ₹1,00,00,000 (5 lakhs - 1 crore)
- Motor Insurance (Car IDV): ₹3,00,000 - ₹50,00,000 (3 - 50 lakhs)
- Home Insurance: ₹25,00,000 - ₹2,00,00,000 (25 lakhs - 2 crores)

Premium Ranges:
- Term Insurance (₹1 crore): ₹8,000 - ₹20,000/year
- Health Insurance (₹10 lakh): ₹10,000 - ₹25,000/year
- Motor Insurance (Car): ₹8,000 - ₹50,000/year
- Home Insurance: ₹3,000 - ₹15,000/year
